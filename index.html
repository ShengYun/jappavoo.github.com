Research - Pursuing Future Computer Systems

My interest is in pursuing how we will advance the capabilities of computers in the coming decades, such that new systems continue to dwarf the power of systems that preceded them. I am pursuing this along two dimensions. The first, is evolutionary in nature and the second, longer-term, is deconstructive and basic science in nature.

The first aspect of my research continues to look at the evolution of large scale computers systems and their efficient use. In project Kittyhawk we began to explore conjectures about cloud-computing as an evolution to market-driven global scale computers and computation. In particular we explored the viability of advanced general purpose data-center scale systems that evolve from today's super-computers. In manner, howerver, where the usage is not only unique because of the system massive scale and advance integrated communications but also its ability to elastically permit the allocation and deallocation of raw physical nodes in sub-second times. We view such underlying systems as forming the basic resource layer of a Scalable Eastic Systems Architecture (SESA). We are now looking at the role of operating systems software on top of such a resource layer. We have identified a new elastic OS and application model, called the MultiLibOS, and are constructing and evaluating a specific research prototype called EbbOS.

During my work exploring scalable software models for large scale systems I have developed a machine model called "Programmable Smart Machines" that combines classical logic-based computation with pattern matching/statistical methods. We conjecture that this model can yield systems that can be programmed easily with little or no regard to the system's physical structure, but whose performance and function improve automatically with system size and ``experience''. This conjecture and the various avenues for pursuing it forms the second dimension of my work. I believe this conjecture is a rich area of study that may have the long-term potential of disruptive results that could suggest a new kind of general purpose programmable intelligent system that can be practically realized.

Teaching - Clarity, passion and a balance between theory and practice

I have three aspirational goals with respect to teaching. The first is to share with students the joy and excitement of learning something new while helping cultivate the skills for self-directed discovery. The second is to help students develop the technical skills necessary to achieve their goals and the third is to challenge students to find the beauty in all subjects.
